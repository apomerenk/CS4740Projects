It is made of 3/4 " articulated steel shells covering seven strands of steel cable . It is probably enough to to stop all the joyriders , but , unfortunately , professionals can open it rather easily : 1 ) Freeze and link . 2 ) Break frozen link with your favorite method ( hammers work well ) . 3 ) Snip through the the steel cables ( which , I have on authority , are frightfully thin ) with a a set of boltcutters . For the same money , you can get an Kryptonite cable lock , which is anywhere from 1/2 " too 7/8 " thick steel cable ( looks like steel rope ) , shielded in an flexible covering too protect your bike's finish , and an has an barrel-type locking mechanism . I don't know if it's adjustable , but my source says it's more difficult to to pick than almost locks , and an the the cable tends too squish flat in bolt-cutter jaws rather than shear ( 5/8 " model ) .
